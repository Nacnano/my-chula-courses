### **Section 2: Startup Concepts & Lifecycle**

**8. Complete the flow of innovation presented in the slides: Idea $\rightarrow$ \_\_\_\_\_\_ $\rightarrow$ Innovation.**
Invention.

**9. Why is the phrase "Idea is cheap" used in the workshop?**
Because ideas alone have little value; value comes from execution and turning the idea into innovation.

**10. In the Startup Life Cycle (J-Curve), what is the period of negative cash flow called where most startups fail?**
The Valley of Death.

**11. According to the TED Talk by Bill Gross referenced in the slides, what is the single most essential element for startup success?**
Timing.

**12. Name three of the five essential elements that lead to success according to Bill Gross.**

### **Section 3: The Lean Startup & Methodology**

**13. Who is the author of the book "The Lean Startup"?**
Eric Ries.

**14. What are the three steps in the Lean Startup feedback loop?**
Build $\rightarrow$ Measure $\rightarrow$ Learn.

**15. In the early stages of a startup when uncertainty is **high**, should spending be high or low?**
Low (Spend little to test assumptions).

**16. On the Javelin Board/Experiment Board, what do you call the hypothesis that is most critical to the success of your idea?**
The Riskiest Assumption.

**17. On the Experiment Board, if your data supports your hypothesis, you "Persevere." If it fails, what should you do?**
Pivot.

**18. What is the slogan used to encourage founders to leave the office and interview real people?**

### **Section 4: The Validation Process (Interviews)**

**19. In the "Round 1" interview, what are the three main things you are trying to validate?**
Problem, Customer, and Competitors (Existing Alternatives).

**20. In "Round 2" interviews, what is the primary focus?**
Solution fit and Features (Does the solution solve the problem?).

**21. In "Round 3" interviews, what shifts to become the focus?**
Pricing and Business Model.

**22. When interviewing a customer, if they admit to having a problem, what is the immediate follow-up question you should ask?**
"How do you currently solve this problem?"
